World's Richest YouTubers in 2022

That's chump change compared to some of the massive purchases these UK YouTubers made in March of 2019 $500,000, custom-made chain modeled after the Dragon Ball Super character furious the chain contains over 2000 stones it's 550 hours of craftsmanship.

From his two channels, KSI likely makes between $1.2 and $3.2 million annually.



Logan Paul


One of the most controversial YouTubers in the world started recording for youtube at age of 24. After that started doing everything from starring in movies to hosting a YouTuber Olympics having the number one podcast in the world and the largest non-professional boxing match in history. Logan has proven himself to be an incredibly resourceful businessman and his hard work is more than paid off the American

YouTuber lives in a gorgeous $6.6 million man Encino CA with seven bedrooms a full gym and a huge podcasting studio inside. The number one podcast in the world can also put $225,000 into converting a regular old yellow school bus into his signature cool bus while he has a ton of money coming in.

From brand deals touring and his hugely popular maverick merch. Logan Paul's channel likely brings in between $666,000 and $1.8 million a year.



Jake Paul


The younger brother of logan paul jake paul made a name for himself. His channel grew from nothing to 5,000,000 subscribers in a matter of six months. A feat that at the time had never been done before. American YouTuber shows that he's branched out from YouTube into getting paid the big bucks for speaking engagements at massive business conventions around the world. Combine that with his hugely popular music career and wildly successful merge lines Jake Paul is pulling in the big. Jake has a liking for buying nice cars such as his $132,000 Tesla Model X and $350,000 Lamborghini Oricon are the crown jewels in his prize collection while most of his money comes in through his merch line.

His YouTube channel brings from $3milion up to $3.4 million a year.

Imagine going from being a sub 1K YouTube channel to one of the three most well-known gaming channels on the planet. With a massive audience of nearly 23 million subscribers and counting frequently collaborates with the other big gamers of the famous trio Markiplier and PewDiePie. Generous Irishman doesn't like to spend his money to flex and prefers to use his influence to raise money for various charities raising billions of dollars over the years. He helps mental health awareness by providing clean water to third-world countries and famously raising $225,000 for the American foundation for suicide prevention during a single eight-hour charity live stream.

His YouTube channel earns an estimated $3 to $8 million a year from ads alone.

David Dobrik


Slovakian vlogger is a respectable 13.2 million subscriber channel. David lives in a $2.5 million mansion in Los Angeles which he says is not too big not too small not too modern and not too old perfectly balanced as all things should be except for that price.

David doesn't just use his millions to spoil himself though and famously prefers to spend it to make his friends happy. Then record their reactions and post them on YouTube to make it back. This channel brings in between 4.8 and $13 million a year All in all is living proof that dreams do come true.



Dude Perfect


OK, so this one isn't so much a YouTuber as it is a team of YouTubers but trust me they've earned a spot on this list.

Old college roommates from Texas were able to win over the hearts and minds of the entire world when they first broke out on the YouTube scene. With a current subscriber base of over 45 million dudes perfect is one of the largest YouTube channels on the planet, the team has also ventured outside YouTube and has their show the dude perfect show on Nickelodeon and CMT which has been running since 2016. The group purchased a massive 35,000-square-foot warehouse in Frisco TX.

Their huge channel breaks in between $5 milion and $13.2 million a year even split five ways that are a lot of money.



Ninja Tyler


Famous streamer ninja makes a huge impact on the platform originally starting as a gaming streamer on twitch his YouTube channel is home to the highlights of his gaming career and life. He holds an impressive 22.8 million subscribers arguably the most celebrity to come from the Internet Ninja was the first esports contender to ever be featured on the cover of Sports Illustrated magazine. He's done brand deals with massive companies like epic games Samsung Uber Adidas and Microsoft.

Dollars in stream donations alone ninja earns over $250,000 a month and maintains live audiences of hundreds of thousands at a time. His YouTube channel brings in a passive half a million to 1.3 billion dollars a year. In 2019 ninja made a deal with Microsoft to leave twitch and start streaming exclusively on their debut streaming platform mixer earning him somewhere around$100 million.



Mr. Beast


Currently one of the most famous names on YouTube exploded onto the YouTube scene by giving away thousands of dollars.

For years to almost no views the North Carolina-born YouTubers struck it big in 2017. When some smart investments in cryptocurrency made him an overnight millionaire. He didn't find it fun spending the money on himself. However Mr beast found more joy in giving it all away.

Mr beast earns most of his money or rather the money he gives away from merch sales and brand deals as well as the revenue he makes from views on his videos. He regularly pulls in 10s of millions of views per video and has so far amassed an audience of over 24 million subscribers. Because of these huge numbers his channel alone around $6.2 milion to $17 million a year.

Money and fame haven't changed Felix much because he lives in a humble house in Brighton England and drives an average car.

YouTuber called an estimate of his net worth of $40 million a little low. This channel pulls in millions of views frequently at the top of the trending page although not so much in America for some reason.
