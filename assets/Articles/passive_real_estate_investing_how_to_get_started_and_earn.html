Passive Real Estate Investing: How to Get Started and Earn?

August 31,2022

Author

Ilija Blazhevski

As a result of watching popular reality television series, a large number of American citizens are under the impression that investing in real estate consists of buying houses, making a few superficial upgrades to them, and then selling them for a profit.

There are others who believe that the one and the only method to amass wealth is to make investments in real estate and then use those properties as rental properties year after year. Both of these approaches to investing in real estate are completely legitimate. Having said that, these aren't the only choices you have before you.

Both of these strategies for investing in real estate are extremely innovative. The real estate market offers a diverse selection of opportunities for "passive" investments, which do not require intensive labor or specialized knowledge to be successful.

This article will go over the three most typical approaches to investing in real estate on a passive basis. If you were to ask a group of real estate investors to describe active versus passive real estate investing, you might probably anticipate receiving a wide variety of responses.

Due to our previous involvement in the industry, we are able to divide real estate investments into three primary categories.




Active Real Estate Investing

Taking an active involvement in the process of purchasing, developing, managing, or enhancing the properties in issue is required in order to be considered an active participant in active real estate investing.

Included in this category are residences that are bought with the goal of sprucing them up and selling them off as quickly as possible. Also included in this category is the process of purchasing rental properties with the intention of managing them on one's own.




Passive Real Estate Investing

One example of passive real estate investing is providing financial support to a business that acts on the investor's behalf in the management of real estate holdings.

REITs and real estate partnerships are two examples of these types of passive investments. The primary concept here is that passive investing does not require much involvement on your part.




Active Real Estate Investing With Passive Management

Real estate investments that are passively managed nonetheless come with a number of advantages, despite not being actively handled. Investigating and ultimately purchasing real estate for the purpose of renting it out is a very active investment approach.

However, if you have a property manager on retainer, you will be able to adopt a more hands-off approach to managing the day-to-day activities of the facility. The majority of the money that we have available for real estate investments is currently held in the third choice.

Because of our own experiences, we can witness the fact that investing in buy-and-hold rental homes is never a fully passive endeavor.



Types of Passive Real Estate Investments

There are a lot of hands-free approaches to real estate investing that can be pursued. Investors are able to pool their resources and buy commercial real estate through the use of real estate investment trusts (REITs).

Through the usage of real estate investment trusts, one has the opportunity to invest in a wide variety of commercial real estate properties. Some examples of these properties include apartment complexes, office buildings, warehouses, and shopping centers.

REITs are a fantastic choice to consider when wanting to produce passive income from real estate investments. In most cases, a firm is required to hand over at least 90 percent of its taxable income to its shareholders. Should this be the case, they would be exempt from paying taxes on their corporate revenue.

In addition to that, the real estate sector is home to a variety of different companies that are traded on public exchanges. Examples include the construction business, which includes companies such as the Howard Hughes Corporation, and the real estate market's ancillary service providers, such as Zillow and Realogy.

Other examples include the automotive industry, which includes Ford Motor Company. You also have the option of investing your money in companies that are not primarily concerned with real estate but do have a sizeable amount of it in their possession.

Macy's is a good example because, in addition to its other locations, it has a big flagship store in Manhattan as well as other assets. The unique form of passive investment known as crowdfunding has recently become popular in the real estate market. On platforms such as Crowdstreet and RealtyMogul, investors are able to pool their resources and invest collectively in the sale of real estate.

Crowdfunding could be used by a developer, for instance, to purchase an apartment complex, renovate it, and then sell it for a profit. Crowdfunding has a number of drawbacks, the most notable of which are its low liquidity and the requirement that a successful campaign is dependent on a single item.

Despite this, there is a possibility that the prospective payoff will be very high. In conclusion, if you want to invest in real estate but take a hands-off approach, you might want to consider forming a partnership with other investors who have the same interests as you do.

To provide you with an illustration, one of our investments in real estate is a limited liability company (LLC), which is owned jointly by the three of us and two other investors. We are the only one who actively participates in the process of purchasing and managing real estate; the other two are only investors.




Benefits of Passive Real Estate Investing

Those who aren't particularly knowledgeable about the real estate market can nevertheless participate in passive real estate investing. In order to get started, all you really need is a simple understanding of how to analyze financial statements.

When compared to active real estate investing, passive real estate investing requires significantly less time commitment. Evaluating retail real estate investment trusts (REITs) is a more productive use of your time, for example, when contrasted with the amount of time spent on the administration of a retail property.

One can get their feet wet in passive real estate investing with very little to no initial investment capital. Significant returns can be obtained from even a modest investment in a real estate investment trust (REIT) or other real estate stock, and an investment of a few thousand dollars can get you involved in a crowdfunding real estate project.

You will not be able to buy a house outright that is suitable for your requirements unless you have tens of thousands of dollars available to you. Investing in real estate on a passive basis allows you to put your money to work in ways that would not be conceivable under any other circumstances.

Take into consideration the fact that very few people have the financial resources necessary to purchase a high-rise urban apartment complex. However, due to developments such as real estate investment trusts (REITs) and crowdfunding platforms, it is now possible to invest money in this industry.




Reasons to Take a More Active Approach

The major consideration is the possibility of increased profits. The majority of passive real estate investments have a lesser potential return on investment compared to either purchasing a property to rent out or engaging in a fix-and-flip strategy.

You may expect to expend a great deal more work and devote a great deal more time to this endeavor. Active real estate investing can result in large profits, but only for those investors who have the time, knowledge, and willingness to be actively involved in the process.

Additionally, active investment strategies provide you control over your portfolio, which is a significant advantage. It is entirely up to you to decide anything and everything that is connected to the rental homes and fix-and-flip properties that you own.

Having the ability to make judgments is a significant advantage, but only if you are also equipped with the necessary level of expertise.

In the end, there is no one answer that is invariably correct. In our experience, the vast majority of investors are best suited for investments in real estate that need little to no active management. The vast majority of people do not possess the necessary level of competence to successfully buy or sell a home on their own.

They also lack the time necessary to actively participate in the real estate investment process. Active real estate investing, on the other hand, could be a good option for you if you want to take a more hands-on approach to your investments and are willing to take on more of a learning curve.

Naturally, if this is your first time doing something, we believe it is advisable to take things at a gradual and steady pace. Your plan for real estate investing can benefit from taking a combination of active and passive techniques, depending on the circumstances.

Personally, we have almost the same amount of money invested in real estate investment trusts as we have in real estate itself. This is also true for our business. In addition to this, we have a property manager who handles all of the routine responsibilities.

When developing a plan for investing in real estate, it is important to take into account not only your goals as an investor but also your level of comfort with taking risks and the amount of time you have available.

Take into consideration the aforementioned factors before settling on an active or passive strategy for investing in real estate.


What Are the Risks of Passive Income Investing?

You should be aware that there is always a risk that you could end up losing money if you invest in real estate. This is something that you should keep in mind whenever you make financial investments.

Every single investment that is made in a prospective source of passive income puts the investor in jeopardy of the ever-present possibility of suffering a full loss of money. This is the case regardless of the amount of wealth that is invested.

There could be a number of reasons behind a decline in the price of a stock or real estate investment trust (REIT). It's possible that problems with the underlying asset are one of these reasons (the firm whose shares you own or the real estate portfolio of the REIT), while it's also possible that a general slump in the market is one of these variables. It is possible that either way, you will lose money on your investment.

Because of this, conducting your own research prior to making any kind of investment, regardless of whether the investment will be active or passive and regardless of whether it will be in real estate or another asset class, is of the utmost importance.

This is true whether the investment will be in real estate or another asset class. There is not a single method of investment that guarantees a profit or even the safety of one's starting cash.

Your own independent investigation, on the other hand, could point you in the direction of investments that are not only risk-free but also offer the opportunity for a return on investment.
