12 Hidden Costs of Owning an Electric Car

September,27,2022

Author

Dragan Stojanovski


As the price of gasoline continues to climb, an increasing number of consumers are thinking about making the switch to electric vehicles. It would be tempting to make the switch, given that gasoline prices are now hovering around $4 a gallon.

When 2021 rolled around, almost 70 percent of all-electric cars purchased in the United States were Teslas. Tesla's market share is decreasing due to competition from various companies, including Nissan, Ford, and General Motors, amongst others (EVs).

Should you get an electric vehicle, then? Before making a move to electric power, it is important to consider the one-time as well as ongoing costs that are related to this new method of energy provision.



1. Higher Purchase Price

Even before you get the keys in your hand, the purchase price of some electric vehicles may be higher than the price of similarly equipped conventionally powered automobiles (or key fob). According to the data provided by Kelley Blue Book, the typical cost of an electric vehicle in November 2021 was $56,437.

This was a significant increase compared to the median price of gasoline and electric autos, which was $46,329. The increased cost is a result of both increased demand as well as additional electric vehicle technologies.



2. Higher Registration Fees

Occasionally, states will levy an additional tax on top of the price of fuel in order to contribute funds toward the unavoidable upkeep and repairs that will need to be performed on the nation's roadways.

However, because electric cars (EVs) do not need petrol, their owners might not be contributing as much as they could to funding infrastructure. In order to make up for this revenue loss, some jurisdictions have begun increasing the amount that owners of electric cars must pay to register their vehicles. Depending on the state, the annual membership fee might be anywhere from $50 to $200. Find out how much more you'll have to pay from the state where your automobile is registered by contacting the DMV.



3. Charging on the Road

After investing in an electric vehicle and installing a charging station in your home, it is probable that your regular electricity bill may go up. But the real jolt can come if you try to speed up when you're behind the wheel.

Access to public charging stations can cost anything from free to many dollars, depending on the location. In addition to this, some jurisdictions allow service providers to charge customers based on the total number of minutes they use the service; however, in other states, service providers are only permitted to charge consumers based on the total number of kWh used by their clients.

For instance, Blink's charges can range anywhere from four cents to six cents per minute or from thirty-nine cents to seventy-nine cents per kilowatt hour, depending on the location of the charging station.



4. Higher Premiums

There is a possibility that the insurance rates for electric vehicles will be higher than those for traditional gas-powered vehicles. The cost of insurance for an electric vehicle is typically 25% higher than that of an equivalent policy for a vehicle powered by gasoline. There is a possibility that insurance premiums will be higher for electric vehicles because of the increased costs connected with their maintenance and replacement.

It is essential that you do some shopping around for electric vehicle (EV) insurance if you want to reduce the amount of money you spend on coverage. Here are several ways that you might save as much as $500 annually on the cost of your auto insurance rates.



5. Battery Replacement

A common concern among owners of electric vehicles is that their vehicles will not have the battery power to bring them to their destination.

The good news is that developments in battery technology are increasing the distance that can be driven on a single charge of the battery. It is likely that you will need to purchase a replacement battery for your electric vehicle every eight years or 100,000 miles. Although it would appear to be a lot right now, the price of a new battery might range anywhere from $5,600 to $20,000, depending on the model.



6. Unlocking Add-Ons

EV manufacturers such as Tesla may stress the benefits of additional features such as self-driving and acceleration boost, but it is important to note that these additions do not come cheap. It is possible that drivers will be required to pay a fee of several thousand dollars to access these computerized capabilities.

For example, the fully automated driving option in Tesla vehicles can be activated for a price of $10,000.



7. Higher Repair Costs

As a result of the fact that certain repairs are often more expensive on an electric automobile than they are on a gasoline-powered vehicle, the overall cost of repairs can go up. The annual average cost of repairing a Tesla is $832, which is significantly higher than the average cost of repairing all car types and models, which is $652.

If there is a shortage of components, the cost of fixing a broken part can soar, which would be a bad thing. It's likely that getting someone to fix your car will be just as challenging as locating them in the first place.

In contrast to mechanics who are knowledgeable about internal combustion engines, it may be more difficult to find skilled individuals working on electric vehicles. There is also the possibility that the dealership or repair shop you take it to is not authorized to work on vehicles of your particular make and model.



8. Home Charging Station

With the assistance of a fast charger, rapidly recharging your vehicle's battery is an easy task to accomplish. Nevertheless, the cost of installing a charger at home can be high. The cost of installing an electric vehicle charger in your home might range from more than $1,500 all the way up to more than $2,000, depending on a number of criteria, such as labor costs, building codes, and approvals.

Because you are required to select a charger that was made specifically for your vehicle, it is possible that your ability to search around for a charger will be restricted as a result. The cost of hiring an experienced electrician to install automobile chargers and perhaps upgrading your home's electrical infrastructure so that it can allow rapid charging is the prospective expense.



9. Temperature Issues

It's possible that a person who lives in a place like California, where the weather is nearly constantly bright and warm (about 75 degrees), won't have any issues with charging their device. On the other hand, if you live in an area that experiences a more abrupt change in temperature from cold to hot, your battery's ability to charge could be negatively affected by the temperature.

You may need to shell out more cash to pay for charging fees that are flexible enough to accommodate changes in the weather.



10. Charging Time

When determining whether or not to acquire an electric vehicle, it is important to factor in the worth of your time, despite the fact that assigning a monetary value to it might be challenging.

Even though your electric vehicle's battery can be recharged using a fast charger in less than thirty minutes, this is still a significant amount of time longer than it takes to fill up a gas-powered vehicle. If you aren't utilizing a fast charger, this period might easily balloon up to seven, eight, or even twelve hours.



11. Maintenance

Despite the fact that electric vehicles (EVs) don't need regular maintenance, like having their oil changed or having their engine air filters replaced, they do occasionally need repairs. Just like any other vehicle, it needs routine maintenance, such as having the oil changed, having the tires rotated, and having the alignment checked.

The requirement to replenish fluids on a regular basis is an unanticipated cost that comes with owning an electric vehicle. As part of routine maintenance, the brake fluid should periodically be flushed, and the coolant in the thermal management system should be replaced. Possibly, the transmission fluid in certain models will need to be replaced at regular intervals.

Even though the very low costs of maintaining an electric vehicle may come as a pleasant surprise, they are still significantly lower than the costs of maintaining a conventional vehicle throughout the duration of the vehicle's lifetime. How significant a difference does each one truly make? The cost to maintain a Toyota RAV4 over one year is approximately $1,000, while the same cost for a Tesla Model 3 is less than $190.



12. Expensive Add-Ons

However, these capabilities do not come cheap and come with additional expenditures. Autopilot and better acceleration are just two of the perks that Tesla and other manufacturers of electric vehicles are quick to point out.

It is likely that drivers will need to spend several thousand dollars more in addition to the standard price in order to gain access to these features. In Tesla's case, the self-driving mode's activation carries a price tag of $10,000. This is just one example. This is just one illustration among many.

This number has grown over the course of time, which is one of the factors that has caused the price of a used Tesla to rise. Despite the fact that the technology for self-driving cars is not yet in the position where it needs to be, Tesla maintains that you are paying for future potential when you purchase one of their vehicles.




Are Electric Cars Cost-Effective?

People frequently fail to account for all of the expenses involved in owning and operating an electric vehicle. The insurance premiums are more expensive, the utility rates are all over the place, and there is still maintenance and repair work to be done. The initial cost is the most significant barrier to entry because, depending on the model, it can be thousands of dollars more expensive than purchasing an equivalent gas-powered vehicle.

On the other hand, if you intend to retain an electric vehicle for more than a few years, you should anticipate a reduction in the total amount of money spent on fuel over time. In addition, you won't have to be as concerned about the influence your automobile has on the environment as you would be if you were driving a car that was fueled by fossil fuels.



Bottom line

Suppose you are considering purchasing an electric vehicle. In that case, it is a good idea to investigate the expenses you might not instantly recognize. Purchasing an electric vehicle (EV) does, however, come with several advantages, the most notable of which is a sizeable cut in the amount of money you would normally spend on gasoline. Check your finances and think about what could happen if you do this. It is ultimately up to you to decide whether you want to switch to electric power or keep using conventional power.

There are a number of obvious benefits to driving an electric vehicle as opposed to a gas-powered automobile; nevertheless, there are also a number of hidden expenses that need to be considered, which could result in a significantly larger overall price. When deciding whether or not to purchase an electric vehicle, it is essential to take into consideration the additional costs that are involved with the transaction.

These costs include the higher initial cost, as well as the cost of repairs, charging fees, and the cost of replacing the battery. In spite of the larger initial investment, you will wind up spending less on gas and repairs over the course of the vehicle's lifetime.

The purchase of electric vehicles is supported by financial incentives and rebates from a number of states as well as the federal government. The number of people worried about their actions' effect on the environment drives up the demand for electric automobiles.

Many motorists are making the conversion to electric vehicles despite the fact that the upfront cost of an electric vehicle may be higher than that of a gas-powered vehicle. This is because of the long-term financial and environmental benefits of electric vehicles.
