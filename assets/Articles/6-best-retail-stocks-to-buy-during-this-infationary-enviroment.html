6 Best Retail Stocks to Buy During This Inflationary Environment

August 22,2022

Author

Dragan Stojanovski

Throughout the entirety of this year, rising expenses have been the primary concern of investors. Those who are concerned about the long-term impact that inflation will have on their investments should be aware, however, that there are certain areas of the market that can continue to give stability even as prices continue to climb.

Among the best stocks to hold for inflation are those offered by businesses that stand to profit from the present economic, financial, and global trends.

Consumers, for instance, suffer losses as a result of inflation, whilst certain businesses, such as those involved in the production of food and energy, stand to gain.

While some retail establishments may be severely impacted by inflation, others may be able to differentiate themselves in order to attract customers and investors despite the challenging economic climate.

Following is a list of the top investments to safeguard one's wealth against inflation. The names that have been listed stand out as outstanding candidates for a variety of reasons. To begin, the majority have optimistic projections of their future profits made by specialists in the field.

Although you might not agree with their forecasts, it is difficult to dispute the connection between the fluctuation of stock prices and the opinions of financial specialists. In addition, each of these organizations is a significant contributor to the overall financial security of the country.

Along with a robust financial sheet, evidence of this can be found in the form of rising dividends, a commitment to share repurchases, or both. Alternatively, this can be seen as a combination of the two.



CF Industries


The current status of the planet seems to be somewhat precarious. CF Industries (CF, $103.50), a manufacturer of fertilizer ingredients such as ammonia and urea, is not any less perilous than other stocks, but it does provide the kind of delight that comes from knowing you are invested in the right stock at the right time.

Fertilizer is in high demand all across the world, yet there are not enough supplies to meet that need. The company has benefited as a result of this development. In September of 2020, the price of anhydrous ammonia fertilizer was slightly higher than $400 per tonne. In July of 2022, the price per tonne reached an all-time high of over $1,600.

Because of the correlation between the continued high cost of natural gas and the significant role that natural gas plays in fertilizers, these lofty readings are likely to remain for the foreseeable future. This is because natural gas continues to play a significant role in the production of fertilizers.

When compared to the pricing in Europe, which are approximately twenty percent higher, CF realizes savings of almost eighty percent when it purchases natural gas in the United States. Even though the United States is a major supplier of fertilizer, not all producers or suppliers of fertilizer have access to the resources that it has to offer.

The increase in operating margin is another key factor, as it went from approximately 31% in the first half of 2021 to over 58% in the first half of 2022. The increasing sales volume of CF Industries is directly correlated to the dramatic increase in the company's profit margins.

The price of one of the most promising stocks related to inflation has increased by more than 137% over the course of the previous year and is currently trading near its all-time high; this may cause an astute investor to question whether or not they have missed the opportunity. It's possible, but it's highly unlikely.

Nevertheless, investors who are in it for the long haul may be able to overlook the emotional turbulence in the market and instead concentrate on the numbers rather than the market's performance. In addition to significant operational leverage at increasing sales volumes, the company has grown its dividend by an average of 26% per year over the course of the last decade (as reported by Value Line), and it is repurchasing shares at a quick rate.



Costco Wholesale


Costco Wholesale (COST, $554.53) is a favorite among investors for a good reason: the company's management is so skilled at maximizing profits that it can do so in both prosperous and adverse economic climates.

This makes the stock an appealing option for investors. Even better, at least for the time being, is the fact that the company's stock has produced a slow performance so far this year, significantly easing investor anxieties about purchasing at the peak of one of the stocks with the strongest potential returns on inflation.

However, there is a catch: Costco trades at a price-to-earnings (P/E) multiple that is greater than its peers' and pricing for the goods it offers to customers, particularly now, in a period of historic inflation.

This is because Costco trades at a price-to-earnings multiple that is greater than its peers' and pricing for the goods it offers to customers. During the nine months that ended on May 8th, the gross margin (sales minus operating expenses) only decreased by 70 basis points or 0.70 percent. The rise in sales was far higher than 16%.

Second, Costco has a track record of increasing its dividends. Despite the fact that the dividend yield is only 0.7%, investors may take solace in the fact that it has climbed at an average annual rate of 13% over the course of the previous decade.

As dividend growth builds over the course of time, investors that have a very long time horizon could potentially witness exceptional cash-on-cash gains.



Devon Energy


Devon Energy (DVN, $68.51) has a compelling case since it is succeeding in a favorable environment and possesses a competitive advantage. The second quarter saw a growth in revenues of approximately 138 percent year over year and a nearly sevenfold increase in earnings per share compared to the same period in the prior year.

Numerous factors can be responsible for the undeniably favorable financial results which have been achieved.

To begin, current financial comparisons are made against the backdrop of oil prices that reached above $120 per barrel in the second quarter. This represents a rise of about 60% year-to-date from the beginning of the year.

DVN has increased its production, and they have the resources to continue doing so even at its present pricing point. In the most recent quarter that was disclosed,

Devon delivered almost 300,000 barrels of oil per day (MBbls/d), which was approximately 4% higher than the company's midpoint expectation.

As is the case with the majority of oil companies, the more oil a company pumps out, the more money that company makes. Devon reported a "field level" operating profit of $60 for the second quarter, which was an increase from the $42 reported at the end of 2017.

When it comes to the oil sector, size does matter. Companies like Devon that are committed to increasing their share count through buybacks also often demonstrate a commitment to increasing their dividend payout.

Devon announced a dividend payment of $1.55 per share in connection with the release of its financial report for the second quarter.

Notably, due to its 9.1% dividend yield, DVN is currently regarded as one of the top inflation stocks for investors who are interested in generating money. It would appear that investors have more and more doubts about the company's ability to maintain its current levels of oil output and income.

Devon, on the other hand, maintains a positive outlook, and the company just shelled out $1.8 billion to acquire Validus Energy in order to enhance its exposure to the all-import Eagle Ford field.



Pilgrim's Pride


Pilgrim's Pride (PPC, $30.49), which is gaining from the increasing cost of meat and the growing popularity of chicken as a key source of protein around the world, is one company that is benefiting from inflation.

The company's stock price is currently at $30.49. In addition, the cost-to-output ratio for chicken farming has significantly decreased over the past three decades, falling to barely one-third of what it was thirty years ago. This represents a huge leap forward from the situation that existed previously.

PPC is unquestionably on a roll right now. The financial results have exceeded expectations, as evidenced by an increase in the gross margin of more than 3 percentage points and unparalleled growth of the operating margin, which has increased from 0.5 percentage points in 2021 to 10.3 percentage points this year.

The key contributor to the significant rise in operating profit in 2021 was an extensive reduction in operating expenses brought about by the resolution of various legal disputes. This was the primary factor in the impressive increase.

ESG investors, particularly those who place emphasis on the "S" component of the equation, may find the company's history of price fixing and antitrust cases to be of interest.

To this point, in 2022, the performance of stocks has only been satisfactory. The fact that both the top and bottom lines of the company are still showing signs of strength is excellent news for those who are willing to take risks and are hoping to capitalize on inflation by owning PPC stock.

The retail sector is on the road to recovery, which is good news for demand, and customers all over the world are becoming more interested in chicken, which is also good news for the top line of the industry.



Photronics


Photronics (PLAB, $23.62) is a company that specializes in producing photomasks, which are then put to use in the manufacturing of integrated circuits and flat-panel displays. The remaining 25 percent of PLAB's revenue comes from the selling of flat panel display products.

PLAB's end markets are not as dependent on certain industries as those of its competitor Nvidia (NVDA), which has seen its sales projections lowered due to the challenges experienced in the gaming business.

In addition, PLAB is putting a significant amount of stock in much more significant market factors. It is anticipated that China would concentrate a significant amount of attention, as part of its "Made in China 2025" industrial agenda, on the manufacturing and application of integrated circuits and flat-panel displays.

In the meanwhile, it is anticipated that the CHIPS and Science Act of 2022 will lead to an increase in the manufacture of semiconductors in the United States.

Despite the fact that PLAB is a small-cap stock with a market worth of approximately $1.5 billion, the company has been doing exceptionally well financially as of late. During the six months that ended on May 1st, revenues climbed by 26%, but earnings per share increased by almost 200%, reaching 88 cents. The company was able to increase its gross margin by 11 percentage points, which had a major impact on the bottom line, despite the fact that the prices for inputs such as labor, materials, and rent went up.

The PLAB is experiencing tremendous growth, and the company sees an opportunity to tie its expansion to the Made in China 2025 initiative. This is encouraging news for one of the best stocks to buy for inflation, but the unpredictable political atmosphere also presents some risks.



Tapestry Inc. (TPR)


Tapestry, which is the parent company of three upscale fashion companies, including Coach, Kate Spade New York, and Stuart Weitzman, owns them all. This company is responsible for the production of a wide variety of goods, including perfumes, shoes, and handbags, amongst other things.

There is a low probability that Tapestry's high-end clientele will be discouraged by the company's decision to raise prices. Analyst Lorraine Hutchinson predicts that the ongoing restrictions and lockdowns in China will have a negative impact on the results of Tapestry's fiscal fourth-quarter operations, but she believes that the company's fundamentals will continue to be strong.

Hutchinson is of the opinion that the reversal experienced by Kate Spade is a success story and that

Coach is continuing to do well. In addition, it is anticipated that the buyback of company stock will amount to a total of $1.6 billion throughout the fiscal year 2022. Bank of America has a "buy" rating on shares of TPR stock and has set a price target of $55 for the stock. Trading for the day on July 5 finished at $31.34 a share.
