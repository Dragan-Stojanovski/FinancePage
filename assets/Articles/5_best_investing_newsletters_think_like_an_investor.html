5 Best Investing Newsletters: Think Like an Investor

September,19,2022

Author

Dragan Stojanovski


Reading financial newsletters is beneficial for investors of all experience levels. Nobody has enough time to become an expert on every stock that is now available, and there is an infinite number of locations to read about the economy. This is especially true in light of the frequent swings in the values of stocks. By subscribing to a newsletter, you may maintain your awareness of the state of the stock market with very little effort.

There is a large selection of financial newsletters available for your perusal. In this essay, we will talk about the best financial newsletters, offer advice on whether or not you should buy the stocks they recommend, and cover a lot more ground.



What is an Investment Newsletter?

Typical examples of recommended investments found in financial newsletters include stocks that should be bought or sold. The guidance is offered by a well-known investor or group of investors that are respected in the industry.

Investment newsletters frequently include non-recommendatory financial news and updates on the stock market in addition to other types of financial news. Everything presented here is intended to assist readers in better comprehending the current market trends and making investment decisions.



1. Motley Fool Rule Breakers

Rule Breakers by Motley Fool primarily focuses on stocks involved in emerging industries with the potential for significant growth. This service is not concerned with what is now popular but is always on the hunt for the next major stock to invest in. The following six criteria are taken into consideration by the service prior to making a stock recommendation to paying customers:

When entering a new market, the only companies worth investing in are those that have the potential to become market leaders. According to The Motley Fool, the floppy drive business as a whole is experiencing a downturn, even if your company is the market leader in floppy drives.


  • There needs to be an advantage over the competition that can be maintained.
  • The company's stock price must have shown a significant increase in the past.
  • The company's leadership needs to be comprised of competent and self-assured personnel.
  • The level of interest shown by customers must be high.
  • The value of the company ought to be blown out of proportion by the media.

Before recommending a stock to its customers, as you can see, Rule Breakers considers a great deal of information from a variety of sources. Finding well-managed businesses in developing markets that have a competitive edge that can be maintained over time is a primary focus of the service. In addition, if their results are any indicator, it is evident that adhering to their rules is to one's advantage.



2. Seeking Alpha

Seeking Alpha Premium is an investment resource that is both extensive and inexpensive, and it is directed specifically toward advanced and experienced investors. Seeking Alpha Premium is an all-encompassing investment research and recommendation service that offers in-depth analysis of financial news, stocks, and other topics to assist investors in making more informed decisions.

The goal of the service is to help investors maximise their returns on their portfolios. Seeking Alpha provides investors of all stripes with the resources they need, regardless of whether they favour mobile access or want to schedule time for in-depth research and analysis.

Seeking Alpha is the only website that provides free access to everything, including but not limited to the following categories:


  • Scripts of the quarterly conference call for reporting earnings.
  • Ratings and Performance Metrics for Individual Authors on Seeking Alpha.
  • Documents from the bank dating back ten years.
  • Stock price comparisons with other companies in the same industry on a side-by-side basis.
  • Information in great detail, including forecasts of both dividends and profits.


Motley Fool Stock Advisor

The key factor that sets Motley Fool's services apart from its competitors is the nature of the stock-picking suggestions provided. In Stock Advisor's opinion, the best investments are large, well-known firms.

Over 10 years ago, they recommended that their customers invest in companies such as Netflix and Disney, which are now extremely profitable businesses. If you sign up for the service, you will have access to their proposal archive. You will be able to evaluate their effectiveness on your own.

According to the information provided on their website, the Motley Fool Stock Advisor stock subscription service has generated returns of approximately 374% since its inception in February 2002. This figure is calculated by taking into account the average return generated by all of their stock suggestions made over the course of the previous 17 years.

Finding a method that can consistently outperform the market can be something that piques your attention. The Alpha Picks section of Seeking Alpha is one option among several. Alpha Picks is a stock selection service that provides clients with two monthly stock recommendations that the Alpha Picks team believes have the greatest possible upside in terms of price appreciation.

They base their investment decisions not on marketing language or trends but on fundamentals like value, growth, profitability, and momentum. These are the cornerstones of their investment selections. Seeking Alpha employs its own one-of-a-kind, data-driven computer scoring approach to screen firms and give recommendations for investors who choose a more conservative investment strategy known as "buy and hold."

And suppose the results of their backtest are any indicator. In that case, it is evident that their strategy was successful in the past when its approach was simulated: The success rate of the recommendations provided by Alpha Picks was 180 percentage points higher than the success rate of the S&P 500 Index.



Trade Ideas

Trade Ideas is a platform powered by artificial intelligence (AI) and provides users with virtual trading assistance, analysis, and warning services. Price alerts are included in both their Standard and Premium subscriptions, and their TI Swing Picks index email, costing $17 per month each.

This means that you will receive five fresh trading suggestions based on the company's model portfolio each and every Sunday night. These proposals will be emailed to you. In addition, they distribute a free newsletter called Trade of the Week once a week. In each issue, the newsletter highlights a stock suggestion the company's staff made.

The algorithm Trade Ideas uses does a new scan every week to discover unusual trading trends. As seen with GameStop, identifying a high short float percentage can serve as an early warning signal for the company regarding the potential for a short squeeze. Before being made available to users, real people examine and validate the results of scanning.

You will learn what criteria were used to identify the trades included in each email alert, why the service thinks those trades would be successful, and how you may find deals on your own that have comparable qualities to those highlighted in the newsletter alerts. You can subscribe to either their premium email, which costs $17 per month or their free basic newsletter.

This is one of the best elements of their newsletters. Suppose you would like to become more successful in trading stocks. In that case, you should sign up for the newsletters and then think about acquiring a full subscription to the publication.
